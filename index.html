What Is a Snow Day?

A snow day is a day when schools or offices close due to heavy snowfall, extreme cold, or dangerous road conditions. This snow day calculator estimates the likelihood of such closures using weather factors.

How Does the Snow Day Calculator Work?

  • Low temperatures increase snow day chances.
  • Higher snowfall raises closure probability.
  • Strong winds worsen weather conditions.
  • Icy or snow-covered roads increase risk.

Is This Snow Day Prediction Accurate?

This tool provides an estimated probability based on common weather patterns. Actual school closures may depend on local policies and infrastructure.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What snowfall amount usually causes a snow day?

Typically, snowfall above 10–20 cm significantly increases the chance of school closures.

Does temperature matter more than snowfall?

Both are important, but extreme cold combined with snowfall has the highest impact.

Can I use this calculator for any country?

Yes, but results are general estimates and may vary by region.
